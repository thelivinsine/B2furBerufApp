# Genauly: Business Plan and Case Study

_Version 1.1 · June 2026 · Confidential_

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Genauly is a mobile-first web app that helps adult learners break through the intermediate German plateau, the common stall at B1-B2 where people know the basics but cannot yet hold a real conversation with confidence. It builds practical fluency through the situations that actually matter in daily life: a presentation at work, navigating a Behörde appointment, a doctor visit, a job interview, or a difficult conversation with a colleague. It also supports direct preparation for the **telc Deutsch B2 Beruf** and **Goethe-Zertifikat B2** exams, the certificates that gate professional licensing and skilled-worker visa recognition in German-speaking countries. The app is in its early stage and growing: content, scenarios, and features expand continuously toward an ambitious long-term roadmap. The German skilled-immigration pipeline creates a structurally large and motivated user base: ~172,000 work visas were issued in 2024 ([BAMF MoBeMi 2024](https://www.bamf.de/SharedDocs/Anlagen/DE/Forschung/BerichtsreihenMigrationIntegration/MonitoringBildungsErwerbsmigration/mobemi-jahresbericht-2024.pdf)), ~190,000 people enrolled in state-funded Berufssprachkurse ([BAMF BSK 2024](https://www.bamf.de/SharedDocs/Anlagen/DE/Integration/Berufsbezsprachf-ESF-BAMF/BSK-Jahresberichte/bsk-jahresbericht-2024.pdf)), and Germany needs a net influx of ~400,000 migrants per year to sustain its labor force ([IAB](https://iab.de/warum-braucht-deutschland-400-000-migrantinnen-und-migranten-pro-jahr/)). No mainstream language app addresses this audience with situation-based, context-rich, AI-assisted practice at the intermediate level. Genauly starts free to maximize reach, with a paid tier ready to activate.

## 2. Problem and Purpose

### The intermediate plateau

The most common dropout point in language learning is not the beginning. It is the intermediate plateau: learners who can introduce themselves, order a coffee, and understand slow speech, but cannot handle a real-world conversation under pressure. Research on foreign-language acquisition consistently identifies the B1-B2 boundary as the point where progress stalls most frequently, because generic vocabulary and grammar no longer build the contextual fluency needed for real situations ([Common European Framework of Reference, Council of Europe](https://www.coe.int/en/web/common-european-framework-reference-languages)). For German learners in particular, this stall has high practical stakes.

For those in the German immigration pipeline, B2 is not optional. The telc B2 exam costs €150-229 per attempt ([exam center prices](https://ibz-essen.de/telc-deutsch-b2-termine-preise/)), and nurses and doctors need B2 certification as a legal prerequisite for their license to practice ([Make it in Germany](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/de/arbeiten-in-deutschland/gefragte-berufe/pflegefachkraefte)). Mainstream apps like Duolingo and Babbel offer general B2 content but no situation-specific practice, no exam-format dialogues, and no on-demand writing or speaking feedback. A failed exam attempt means another fee and weeks of delay.

### What Genauly does

Genauly is a free learning app for adults who already know some German (around B1) and want to reach genuine, usable fluency in real-life situations. It builds that fluency through contextual practice across everyday scenarios: work presentations, Behörde visits, doctor appointments, job interviews, and more. It also supports preparation for the **telc Deutsch B2 Beruf** and **Goethe-Zertifikat B2** exams. The app is in early-stage development with a long and ambitious content and feature roadmap ahead.

**Auf Deutsch:** Genauly ist eine kostenlose Lern-App für Erwachsene, die schon Grundkenntnisse in Deutsch haben und echte Alltagssituationen meistern wollen: Präsentationen, Behördentermine, Arztgespräche, Vorstellungsgespräche und mehr. Die App befindet sich in einem frühen Entwicklungsstadium mit einem langen und ambitionierten Fahrplan für neue Inhalte und Funktionen.

## 5. Product and Differentiation

Genauly is built around the three things exam-prep learners need most and currently cannot get from one product:

1. **Exam-format speaking practice.** The telc Deutsch-Test für den Beruf B2 oral exam Teil 3 is an explicitly cooperative workplace planning dialogue ("Gemeinsam eine Aufgabe lösen"). Genauly's branching dialogues replicate that format with lifelike workplace scenarios, not generic conversation. ([telc DTB B2 exam guide](https://www.fragamalia.de/post/deutsch-test-f%C3%BCr-den-beruf-b2))

2. **Workplace vocabulary with retention.** 500+ words and ~400 collocations (Nomen-Verb pairs) sourced from telc B2 Beruf word fields (528 words and 396 collocations as of 2026-07, and growing), with spaced repetition so learners remember them under exam pressure.

3. **AI feedback on writing and pronunciation.** One prioritized tip per submission, powered by Claude Haiku with aggressive input caching, keeps cost marginal while giving learners the kind of targeted feedback that previously required a human tutor.

The combination (exam-specific content, workplace context, AI feedback, spaced repetition) does not exist in a single free-to-start product. It is built as a PWA, works offline, syncs across devices via Google sign-in, and fits into a 10-minute lunch-break session.

## 6. Business Model and Pricing

### Freemium, product-led growth

The current model: free and guest-first, no paywall. A `tier` flag, usage counters, and feature flags are already in the codebase, ready for a paid tier without a rewrite.

**Proposed paid tier (assumption, to validate):** €7-9/month or ~€60/year. Rationale:

- Sits well below exam fees (telc B2: €150-229/attempt, Goethe B2: €225 in Germany). Learners who fail once pay more in re-sit fees than a year of Genauly.
- Below Babbel (€9-18/mo) and Lingoda per-class rates (€8-23/class), but above the state-funded free options (giving it perceived value).
- Comparable to Duolingo Super (~€10/mo) and Busuu (~€5-10/mo), which serve a general audience. A focused exam-prep product at the same price with higher relevance should convert better among its specific audience.

**What paid unlocks (suggested):** unlimited AI writing feedback (free tier: 3/day), full dialogue library, progress analytics, downloadable vocabulary lists.

### B2B/B2G angles

1. **BAMF Berufssprachkurs providers (VHS, DAA, private academies):** ~190,000 BSK entrants/year, courses are state-funded (BAMF pays providers). A white-label or referral arrangement with BSK providers could push Genauly to learners who are already motivated and state-enrolled. ([BAMF BSK program](https://www.bamf.de/DE/Themen/Integration/ZugewanderteTeilnehmende/DeutschBeruf/deutsch-beruf.html))

2. **Healthcare employers:** hospitals actively recruit foreign nurses and fund their language training directly. Multiple programs follow an "employer pays" model for the B2 course. ([TalentOrange](https://www.talentorange.com/en/talents/talentorange-programme/)) Per-seat B2B licenses are a natural fit.

3. **Relocation / HR agencies:** companies using the Fachkräfteeinwanderungsgesetz to hire internationally increasingly fund language training as part of onboarding.

4. **Agentur für Arbeit Bildungsgutschein:** individual learners with a Bildungsgutschein can fund accredited courses. Pursuing AZAV accreditation (a medium-term step) would open this channel.

## 8. Go-to-Market and Traction Narrative

### Phase 1: SEO and community (now, zero budget)

The highest-intent searchers are learners actively looking for "telc B2 Beruf Prüfungsvorbereitung", "Redemittel B2 Beruf", "Deutsch-Test für den Beruf B2 üben". These are transactional, low-competition queries where a focused resource ranks well. The app is already deployed on GitHub Pages; adding a content/blog layer (exam guides, practice Modellsätze, Redemittel lists) captures this traffic and funnels it to the app.

### Phase 2: Sprachschule and BSK provider partnerships

The ~4,000+ BAMF-approved Berufssprachkurs providers (VHS branches, private Sprachschulen, DAA) are natural distribution partners. A referral page and teacher toolkit (print-ready vocabulary lists, dialogue scripts) gets Genauly recommended in classrooms with zero paid acquisition cost.

### Phase 3: Healthcare employer and HR partnerships

Target the ~20 largest foreign-nurse recruitment programs and relocation agencies. Offer a per-seat B2B plan. A single hospital with 50 nurse recruits per year paying €60/seat = €3,000 ARR from one partner.

### PWA and app-store distribution

The app already functions as a PWA with offline support. A future React Native wrapper opens the iOS/Android stores for discoverability with no content rework.

## Appendix: Exam naming clarification (important for positioning)

Research confirmed that **there is no certificate called "Goethe-Zertifikat B2 Beruf."** The Goethe-Institut offers only the general **Goethe-Zertifikat B2** (four bookable modules: Lesen, Hören, Schreiben, Sprechen). All work-focused B2 certificates are from **telc**:

- **telc Deutsch B2+ Beruf**: for professionals needing Berufsanerkennung / occupational licensing
- **Deutsch-Test für den Beruf B2 (DTB B2)**: the official BAMF final exam for §45a DeuFöV Berufssprachkurse (the state-funded workplace-German courses), since 2021

The cooperative problem-solving speaking task the app drills ("Gemeinsam eine Lösung finden") maps to **telc DTB B2 / telc B2+ Beruf Teil 3**, not to Goethe B2 (which uses a Vortrag + Diskussion format with no cooperative task). The app's copy and CLAUDE.md should be updated accordingly.

**Corrected positioning:** "Prepare for the **telc Deutsch B2 Beruf** and **Goethe-Zertifikat B2** exams" (or, more precisely: "telc Deutsch B2 Beruf / Deutsch-Test für den Beruf B2 · Goethe-Zertifikat B2").
