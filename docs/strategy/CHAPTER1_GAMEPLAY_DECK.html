Teil 1 · 01 · Die Methode

Four experts, one question

What should the player's hands and mouth actually do in a mission? Four independent personas answered in parallel, then their reports were merged.

P1DaF-Didaktikerin

20 years B1-B2 classroom, telc/Goethe examiner. Activity types by competence and SLA principle, choice design, error handling.

P2Game designer

15 years of 2D RPGs (Pokémon to Papers Please). Interaction verbs beyond multiple choice, choice architecture, bag design, screen grammar.

P3Culture & literature expert

Authentic German text genres, comedy beats that roast the bureaucracy and never the learner, unwritten rules, NPC voices.

P4Market researcher

Web sweep of 11 shipped language games (Wagotabi, Noun Town, Influent, Duolingo Adventures, Terra Alia...): what players praise and pan.

The four reports agreed on one sentence: the answer must be an ACTION in the world before it is ever a sentence in a list.

Teil 1 · 03 · Echte Entscheidungen

Six levers that make a choice matter

Your playtest note "two nice options make no difference" has a design answer. A choice is real only if it moves one of these:

1Resource cost

The plain-polite line costs a turn or Geduld headroom; queuing at 6 a.m. costs Müdigkeit (lower Mut start).

2Time pressure

At hard nodes, dithering itself drips Geduld. Exactly the real counter feeling.

3Information gap

The right move is only knowable if you read the notice or asked first. No more telegraphing the crit on the button.

4Delayed consequence

Goodwill spent early makes the boss pricklier later; the accepted SIM upsell costs money in chapter 3.

5Reactive world

Consequences render as the eyebrow, the sigh, denser Beamtendeutsch. Never a toast.

6Bars first, text second

The two bars and the NPC face are the primary readout of every move.

Error handling, from the examiner: recast instead of red X ("Sie MÖCHTEN sich also ANMELDEN. Gut."), one self-repair window, explicit correction only in the debrief or a Jonas text.

What shipped games prove

Most praisedMost panned

Recall gated on just-taught content in an RPG frame (Wagotabi, Kagami)No production demanded at all (Lingotopia: "very little language learning involved")
Translation withheld until requested (Influent's memory-palace rule)Recognition ceiling: "finished the trilogy, still can't read a sentence"
Real communicative need (Crystallize, the only controlled study: more words AND more fun)Content bugs in the target language (Newcomer): it teaches wrong forms. Our linter is a moat.
  • Gaps nobody owns, we can: real fillable form props, typed free recall inside dialogue, register/politeness as a stat, a comedic composure meter attached to actual language production.
  • Adoption order for G2: 1. hotspots, 2. Automat/keypad, 3. type-under-timer, 4. register consequence caps, 5. one drag-sort renderer (three skins), 6. Leergutautomat, 7. Anzeige decoding, 8. Sprech-Zug.

Spielbuch · 01 · Der Anfang

First launch: who are you?

Before mission 1.1 the game asks four questions. Ninety seconds, no lecture, every answer echoes through the story.

Charaktererstellung G2

1. Dein Name (free text; NPCs use it).

2. Woher kommst du? (free text; flavors small talk: "Ah, aus Brasilien! Weit weg.")

3. Warum Deutschland? Arbeit · Studium · Liebe · Neuanfang. Sets the tone of recurring dream scenes and the chapter-6 ending offer.

4. Wie fühlt sich dein Deutsch an? "Ich verstehe einfache Sätze" (A2/B1.1) · "Ich komme im Alltag klar" (B1.2/B2.1) · "Ich diskutiere gern" (B2+). Seeds the CEFR band of every mission line; changeable anytime in settings.

Intro-Cutscene · Flugzeugfenster G2

"Zwei Koffer. Ein Ticket. Und ein Wort, das du schon kennst: Willkommen."

The reason you picked flashes by as a half-dream (job letter, uni admission, a photo, an empty page). Landing thump. Cut to passport control: mission 1.1 begins. The game teaches by playing, not by tutorial screens: the first document demand IS the bag tutorial.

Spielbuch · 02 · Die Leute

Cast and backstory

You: two suitcases, imperfect German, a reason. There is no villain, only everyday Germany. Continuity comes from six people:

JJonas · dein Freund hier

Met at arrivals (eleven minutes late: "Die Ringbahn hatte mal wieder ihre Laune."). Your wild card: once per mission he rescues, translates or lends, and the game shows HOW. Arc: guide, then real friend, then YOU help HIM.

SFrau Schmidt · Bürgeramt

Chapter-1 boss. Rapid Beamtendeutsch, zero small talk, melts one degree at Konjunktiv II. Recurs at every Amt visit; by chapter 4 she greets you by name. The running gag and the warmest payoff.

AAyşe · Mitbewohnerin (Kap. 2)

Berlin-born, switches Hochdeutsch, Kiezdeutsch and Turkish mid-sentence. Your window into real informal German.

KHerr Krause · Hausmeister

Speaks in complaints. Diplomacy training dummy. Previewed in 1.5 by a single knock from below at 13:05.

WFrau Weber · Chefin (Kap. 4+)

Fair but busy, loves Konjunktiv II. Carries the workplace arc.

NHerr Nguyen · Späti

The town crier: rumors, side quests, Pfand appraisal like an antiques dealer.

Spielbuch · 03 · Deine Hilfen

The help the player carries

Die Tasche DA

Always one tap away in the HUD, shaped like your backpack. Documents are handed over by tapping them; wrong items earn deadpan reactions, never a fail screen. It pulses when someone demands something.

Das Wörterbuch DA

English is a rationed lifeline, not a button: 3 charges per mission, one charge reveals English for the current scene only. Prüfungsmodus (later) simply removes it from the bag.

Jonas' Joker G2

Once per mission: he rephrases the Beamter sentence, lends the missing paper, or steers the small talk back. The rescue always shows how he did it, so it is also the lesson.

Scheitern ist Inhalt DA

No hearts, no lockouts. A lost battle routes into a scaffolded retry that hands you exactly what you were missing, plus the hint that makes round two winnable.

Dein Niveau DA

The CEFR band you picked drives the German of every scene; the FSRS scheduler quietly decides which words come back and when.

Spielbuch · Mission 1.1

Willkommen in Neuland

Environment: Flughafen (Ankunftshalle, Zoll, Bahnsteig). New mechanic taught: the bag and D/E, by playing.

Szene 1 · Passkontrolle · dialogueBattle DA

A gentle first battle: the Beamter has near-infinite Geduld (tutorial). W-Fragen: "Woher kommen Sie? Wie lange bleiben Sie? Arbeit oder Urlaub?" Answers are simple moves; then the first demand: "Ihren Reisepass, bitte." The bag pulses: hand it over. The pass is pre-packed; this teaches Dokument geben safely.

Szene 2 · Zoll · hotspot G2

Two lanes, two compound nouns: "Anmeldefreie Waren" (grün) vs "Anmeldepflichtige Waren" (rot). Tap the lane. Germany sorts you into a category before you say a word.

Szene 3 · Jonas · cutscene DA

He arrives late, takes a suitcase: "Alter, entspann dich, das wird schon. Komm, dein Zug fährt gleich." Warm, casual German against the official register of scene 1.

Szene 4 · Bahnsteig · listen-and-act G2

The Abfahrtstafel shows Zeit, Ziel, Gleis and a small orange "ca. 10 Min später". The announcement calls your train and platform; tap the right Gleis WHILE it plays. Fail path: miss it, next train in 20 minutes, Jonas grins, one slower replay.

Challenge

First contact under mild pressure: understand questions about yourself, act on one announcement.

Loot

Survival pack (Begrüßungen, Richtungen), key item Reisepass, achievement "Angekommen".

Spielbuch · Mission 1.2

Der Fahrkarten-Automat

Environment: Bahnhofshalle, der parodierte DB-Automat. New mechanic: the battle UI, learned safely against a machine.

Szene 1 · Auftrag · cutscene DA

Jonas: "Du brauchst ein Ticket in die Innenstadt. Zone AB. Glaub mir, NUR Zone AB."

Szene 2 · Der Automat · keypad-battle G2

The machine has infinite Geduld but eats coins: every mistake costs. Screens: "Ziel eingeben" (typed), "Tarifzone wählen" (tap AB vs ABC on the ring map, the dartboard nobody explained), "Zahlung". The crumpled-note gag: "Bitte legen Sie den Schein glatt hin." Four times. Behind you a silent queue forms, arms crossed: the pressure bar here is Blicke, not Geduld.

Szene 3 · Entwerten · stamp G2

On the tram the paper ticket still needs the little blue box. Press the stamp. Skip it and you meet the Kontrolleur later (the delayed-consequence hook chapter 5 replays).

Challenge

Decode a machine UI in official vocabulary; numbers and prices under queue pressure.

Loot

Cards "die Verbindung", "die Tarifzone"; the Fahrschein; your first spotted Pfand bottle under a bench (teaser for 1.4).

Spielbuch · Mission 1.3

Die SIM-Karte

Environment: Handyladen im Bahnhofsviertel. New mechanic: register-marked choices with delayed, real consequences.

Szene 1 · Der Verkäufer · dialogueBattle DA

The upsell, 70 percent Denglisch: "Unser Bestseller: die Flex-Flat XL, mit richtig viel Datenvolumen zum Fair-Use-Preis!" Moves: polite-firm refusal ("Nein danke, ich brauche nur Prepaid."), the clarifying question ("Was kostet das monatlich, ohne Rabatt?"), or the soft yes: the trap. Saying yes binds a Vertrag that costs real in-game money in chapter 3 (lever 4: delayed consequence). Small-Talk-Falle when he drifts: "Soll ich Ihnen gleich noch eine Hülle mit dazu packen? Die ist gerade im Sale."

Szene 2 · Das Kleingedruckte · websiteParody DA

The tariff poster: headline price big, truth in size-6 footnote. Tap the line that matters: "Anschlusspreis 19,99 einmalig".

Szene 3 · Ausweispflicht · ask DA

"Zur Identifizierung brauche ich ein gültiges Ausweisdokument. Ist doch nur ein Ausweis, dauert eine Minute."

Bag interaction: hand the Reisepass. Offer the Fahrschein instead and he blinks slowly. Even "no strings attached" has paperwork here: the cultural lesson plays itself.

Szene 4 · Der Vertrag · formCloze DA

Name, Geburtsdatum, tick the right boxes, sign. Loot: key item SIM-Vertrag. Your phone works: Jonas can text you now, which becomes the game's channel for delayed corrections and hints.

Challenge

Resist a professional persuader in a register you must hold: friendly, firm, no rudeness.

Fail path

Accepted upsell = "Flex-Flat-Opfer" ribbing from Ayşe later and a refund side quest in chapter 3. Never a lockout.

Spielbuch · Mission 1.4

Der erste Einkauf

Environment: Supermarkt. New mechanics: hotspot shelf search, the timed checkout, and the Pfand economy opens.

Szene 1 · Die Liste · cutscene + hotspot G2

Jonas texts a German-only list: Brot, Milch, Äpfel, Kaffee. At the door, the Öffnungszeiten sign: "Mo-Sa 8-20 Uhr, Sonntag geschlossen". It is Saturday, 19:12. Run.

Szene 2 · Regal-Suche · hotspot G2

Find the four items by their German labels among distractors. The Kaffee teaches Grundpreis: the big number lies, the small "1,49 € / 100 g" tells the truth. Pick the cheaper one.

Szene 3 · Die Kasse · type-under-timer G2

The legendary scan speed. Clipped cashier German: "Zwölf achtundvierzig. Haben Sie es passend?" · "Beutel brauchen Sie noch?" Type/answer while bagging; fail state is social, not fatal: "Der Nächste kann schon einräumen." Round two runs slower.

Szene 4 · Der Leergutautomat · minigame G2

Feed in the bottles you collected. Each accepted bottle is one micro-retrieval (word match, der/die/das); a miss and the machine spits it back with the dreaded red X and a beep that turns heads. Payout: der Pfandbon. The receipt shows the one line where the store paid YOU: "Pfand 0,25".

Challenge

Read labels fast, survive the checkout, understand a system nobody explains (Pfand, Grundpreis, Sonntagsruhe).

Loot

First Pfandgeld (funds the Späti consumables later), cards "die Quittung", "der Grundpreis", achievement teaser "Pfandmillionär 1/100".

Spielbuch · Mission 1.5

Ein Dach über dem Kopf

Environment: Altbau, the temporary room Jonas arranged. New mechanic: the form ritual. This mission forges the documents the boss will demand.

Szene 1 · Aufwärmen · Kollokations-Paare G2

Drag Nomen onto Verben before the paperwork: Antrag stellen, Formular ausfüllen, Unterschrift leisten, Frist einhalten. Chunks first; the form then reads itself.

Szene 2 · Der Vermieter · dialogueBattle DA

Herr Brandt hesitates over the free signature: "Das bringt mir doch nichts, oder?" Moves: explain the law politely ("Ohne die Bestätigung kann ich mich nicht anmelden. Paragraf 19."), the Nachfrage-Geste, or the crit that wins landlords everywhere: "Ich fülle alles aus. Sie müssen nur unterschreiben." Making it easy for the other person IS the real-life skill.

Szene 3 · Die Wohnungsgeberbestätigung · formCloze + sign DA

Fill the real fields: Einzugsdatum, Wohnungsgeber, Anschrift. Then the signature ritual (drag to sign G2). The document thunks into the bag.

Szene 4 · Die Hausordnung · reading beat G2

Pinned in the Treppenhaus: Ruhezeiten 13-15 und 22-6 Uhr, Mülltrennung, Kehrwoche grid. You vacuum at 13:05. One knock from below. No dialogue, no lecture: Herr Krause has been foreshadowed.

Challenge

Win a reluctant signer with register, then produce your first binding German document.

Loot

Key items Mietvertrag and unterschriebene Wohnungsgeberbestätigung: exactly what mission 1.6 demands at the door.

Spielbuch · Mission 1.6 · BOSS

Die Anmeldung

Environment: Bürgeramt (Website, Wohnung, Wartezimmer, Schalter 2). The boss introduces NOTHING new: it is the exam of everything the chapter taught. Playable today.

Szene 1 · Terminvergabe · websiteParody DA

"Nächster freier Termin: in 8 Wochen." Frist: zwei Wochen. Willkommen in Neuland.

Choice with real stakes (G2 sharpening): waiting risks a Verwarnung side quest; queuing at 6 a.m. costs Müdigkeit (Mut starts lower).

Szene 2 · Packen · walk-and-pick DA

The documents from 1.5 lie in your room; walk to each, name it, bag it. What you leave behind, Frau Schmidt will ask for.

Szene 3 · Wartezimmer · listening DA

Announcements + the number board (107... yours is 112). G2 upgrade: Aufruf abfangen, tap YOUR number the moment it is called.

Szene 4 · Frau Schmidt · dialogueBattle DA

Two bars: her Geduld, your Mut. The Beamtendeutsch status effect: "Ferner benötige ich die Wohnungsgeberbestätigung gemäß Paragraf 19 Bundesmeldegesetz." Both document demands are bag handovers; the wrong item gets: "Das habe ich nicht verlangt." Typed Konjunktiv-II crits: "Wären Sie so freundlich..." / "Hätten Sie vielleicht einen Kugelschreiber?" A pen handed over wordlessly. By her standards, an embrace. Finish quality pays a bonus.

Szene 5 · Das Formular · formCloze DA

The Anmeldeformular, block letters, no mistakes, including the long word you now own: Wohnungsgeberbestätigung.

Fail path

Thrown out = the Rückschlag scene: Jonas hands you the signed papers plus the KII hint, and you walk back in stronger. Failure is content, never lockout.

Loot

Die Meldebestätigung: the key that chapter 3's bank will demand. Chapter 2 unlocks.

Spielbuch · 11 · Die Rampe

One new mechanic per mission

The pacing rule from the research: each mission teaches exactly one new verb; the boss stacks all of them and teaches none.

1.1Willkommen

Teaches: the bag, D/E, listen-and-act. Set piece: acting on your first real announcement.

1.2Fahrkarten-Automat

Teaches: the battle UI, safely (a machine eats coins, not confidence). Set piece: the Tarifzonen riddle.

1.3SIM-Karte

Teaches: register choices that bind later. Set piece: the Flex-Flat XL pitch.

1.4Erster Einkauf

Teaches: timed production + the Pfand loop. Set piece: the checkout speed run.

1.5Dach über dem Kopf

Teaches: the form ritual + the document chain. Set piece: the landlord's signature.

1.6BOSS: Die Anmeldung

Teaches nothing. Demands everything. CEFR peak of the chapter (B1.2), scaffolded retry guaranteed.

Spielbuch · 12 · Der Bauplan

What G2 builds from this book

  • Missions 1.1 to 1.5 as data against these scripts (the boss is live). Everything tagged DA in the scripts needs zero new renderers.
  • Three renderers unlock most of the G2 tags: the stage hotspot layer, the Automat/keypad prop, one drag-sort minigame (three skins: crates, pairs, steps).
  • Systems: Jonas' Joker, the Leergutautomat + Pfandgeld, register consequence caps, Müdigkeit variant of the boss opening.
  • Then the playtest gate (unchanged): 5 to 10 real learners; completion, next-day return, and laughter decide scale-up.

Character creation ships with 1.1 so the chapter plays start to finish as one story. Supabase sync of game state rides the planned G2 migration.
