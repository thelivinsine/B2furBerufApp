Genauly · Spielkonzept

Neuland

Ein Leben auf Deutsch. A story RPG where the loot is language.
Title approved by the founder. City and cast stay proposals you can rename.

▶ Press Start · Design deck · 2026-07-06

01 · Die Idee

Live a life, keep the words

Most language apps are dry, text-based practice with zero personal involvement. Neuland puts the learner inside a story: you arrive in Germany, and every scene, form, argument and small victory attaches new German to something that happened to you.

  • Personal involvement: words are learned inside situations you lived, not decks.
  • Culture, visuals, emotion: scenes teach how Germany works (Pfand, Termine, Ruhezeiten) and make you feel it.
  • Incremental scenes: each mission builds on the last; words return in later scenes so they stick.
  • Built to scale: an authored spine of ~36 missions, then hundreds of generated variants.

There is no villain. The antagonist is everyday Germany: forms, Termine, unwritten rules. Winning means building a life.

02 · Du bist der Held

The player and the premise

You play yourself: two suitcases, a reason for coming, and imperfect German.

  • Character creation: your name, where you come from, and why Germany (job, study, love, fresh start). Your reason colors dialogues and dream scenes through the whole story.
  • Your German level seeds the difficulty (CEFR-banded, from about A2 up). Changeable anytime.
  • The city, „Neustadt": deliberately every-town Germany. S-Bahn spine, Altbau quarter, a concrete Amt, a Büropark, a Kiez with a Späti. Districts unlock chapter by chapter.

04 · So spielt es sich

The core loop

1City map

Pick a story mission, a recurring errand (review in disguise) or a side quest.

2Pack the bag

The mission shows what language it needs. Packing each item is a short retrieval exercise.

3Play the scenes

3 to 6 scenes: dialogues, battles, parody documents and websites, forms, listening moments.

4Take the loot

XP, new word and Redemittel cards, key documents, story progress. Failing spawns a rescue quest, never a lockout.

5The scheduler strikes back

The spaced-repetition engine plays dungeon master: it re-issues mission variants exactly when your words are due for review. "The game made me do the Anmeldung again" and "my Behörde words were due" are the same event.

05 · Interaktion

How the player interacts

  • D and E on every line. Tap D for a simple German explanation, E for English. Tap any word for its card and bag it. Nobody is ever stuck.
  • The input ladder: tap → type → speak. Every moment can be answered by tapping a choice, typing it, or saying it into the mic (opt-in). Higher rungs earn more XP and loot. The game nudges you one rung up when a word is strong.
  • Choices carry register, not right/wrong. Saying „du" to Frau Schmidt is not an error popup. She raises an eyebrow and her patience drops. Consequences live in the world.
  • Documents are playable. Parody Anmeldeformulare, WG ads, ticket machines and booking sites are interactive props. Reading real-life formats IS the gameplay.
  • Listening is diegetic. Voicemails, platform announcements and phone calls play as audio (existing text-to-speech), with replay and a read-along reveal.

06 · Kämpfe sind Gespräche

Battles are conversations

The Pokémon fight, rebuilt for language, without violence. You do not attack anyone; you hold a conversation together.

  • Geduld vs Standing: the opponent's patience against your composure. At zero patience the talk ends („Kommen Sie wieder, wenn Sie alle Unterlagen haben").
  • Moves are cards from your bag, produced on the input ladder. Konjunktiv II politeness lands critical hits; wrong register misfires.
  • Kind status effects: „Missverständnis" (a comprehension check), „Beamtendeutsch" (denser text, D button to the rescue), „Small-Talk-Falle" (steer the topic back).

The Pfand economy: bottles appear everywhere (the park, the office kitchen, after your party). Returning them at the Leergutautomat is a rapid micro-review mini-game: each accepted bottle is one quick retrieval, a miss gets the dreaded red X. The Pfandbon buys your consumables at Herr Nguyen's Späti, so scavenging always pays. Culture included: Einweg 25 ct vs Mehrweg 8 ct, Ayşe's „du wirfst kein Pfand weg" house rule, and bottles left kindly NEXT to the bin for collectors. Achievement: Pfandmillionär (100 bottles, about 25 EUR).

The bag reuses the app's existing categories (theme, level, word class, register), so loot is findable the moment a mission demands it.

08 · Fortschritt

One progress, two views

The app and the game are two windows onto the same learning state. Nothing forks.

  • XP and level: the same pool the app already tracks.
  • Card levels 1 to 5: computed from the memory engine's stability (shipped).
  • The city lights up: the progress page's buildings grow into the game's world map. Mastering a life domain literally turns its lights on.
  • Quests are „Ich kann..." milestones: the existing Can-Do goals become the quest log. Claiming one is a story beat.
  • Review is disguised as life: Germany repeats itself (a new form, another Termin). The scheduler exploits that: recurring missions arrive exactly when your words need refreshing.

09 · Scheitern ist Story

Failure is content, never lockout

  • No hearts, no energy meter, no lives. Both punishing-meter designs on the market visibly failed. You can always play.
  • Losing a battle has story consequences and a scaffolded retry: Jonas texts a hint, the missing phrase slips into your bag, and re-entry is one tap.
  • A missing document spawns its own mini-mission that teaches exactly the gap, then sends you back in stronger.
  • A thin bag scrapes through awkwardly (less XP, funnier scenes); a rich bag wins with flair and bonus loot. Harder later, never stuck later.
  • Prüfungsmodus Opt-in replays any mission with typed answers, timers and no D/E, for better loot. Never the default, and it gives every mission replay value.

10 · Die Geschichte

Six chapters, one chosen ending

An authored spine everyone shares; procedurally generated variants in between. Chapters are life domains, mapped 1:1 onto the app's existing content taxonomy. Every chapter ends on its boss.

K1Ankommen

Airport to Anmeldung. Survive week one.

K2Wohnen

WG hunt, Mietvertrag, Hausmeister, neighbors.

K3Geld & Papierkram

Bank, insurance, Rundfunkbeitrag, the paper dragon.

K4Die Jobsuche

From "what do I want?" to the interview boss.

K5Gesund & Sozial

Arzt, Apotheke, Stammtisch, Verein, your own party.

K6Mein Ziel

You pick the ending: office career, dream home, citizenship, community, or (optionally) an exam.

11 · Kapitel 1 · Bahnhofsviertel

Ankommen

1.1Willkommen in Neuland

Passport control small talk, find your train, meet Jonas. Tutorial for D/E, word-bagging and choices. Loot: survival starter pack.

1.2Der Fahrkarten-Automat

Your first battle is against a machine: the parody ticket Automat and its tariff-zone riddles. Machines have infinite patience but eat coins.

1.3Die SIM-Karte

Phone-shop dialogue, prepaid vs Vertrag, resist the upsell. First contract-reading UI. Key item: SIM-Vertrag.

1.4Der erste Einkauf

Find groceries by German labels, learn the Pfand system, survive the legendary checkout speed (a gentle timed mini-game with a kind fail state). Loot: your first Pfandbon. The Pfand economy opens here.

1.5Ein Dach über dem Kopf

Jonas arranges a temporary room; the landlord needs a signed Wohnungsgeberbestätigung. Your first real form, and the setup for the boss.

BOSSDie Anmeldung Vertical slice

Book the Termin on a parody website, pack your documents, endure the waiting room, face Frau Schmidt in a dialogue battle, and type the Anmeldeformular finale. Reward: the Meldebestätigung, which chapter 3 will demand.

12 · Kapitel 2 · Altbauviertel

Wohnen

2.1Die WG-Suche

Decode parody WG ads („2er-WG, NR, WM vorhanden"), spot the scam listing, and type a short application message.

2.2Die Besichtigung

A casting battle: impress Ayşe and the WG over tea. Small talk, hobbies, the du/Sie decision. The fail path is a worse-but-funny sublet with a retry later.

2.3Der Mietvertrag

Contract reading with trap clauses: Kaution, Nebenkosten, Schönheitsreparaturen. Catch the bad clause or feel it later. Key item: Mietvertrag.

2.4Der Umzug

Borrow a Transporter, navigate the Halteverbot, and build flat-pack furniture from German-only instructions. Imperative grammar as gameplay.

2.5Der Hausmeister

The heater is dead. Win over Herr Krause with your first diplomatic-register moves. Reward: warmth, and his grudging-respect meter appears.

BOSSDie Ruhestörung

Your housewarming was loud and the neighbor complains formally. Mediation battle: apologize without groveling, propose Ruhezeiten, win the Nachbarschaftsfrieden.

13 · Kapitel 3 · Innenstadt

Geld & Papierkram

3.1Das Bankkonto

The bank demands your Meldebestätigung: the chapter-1 key item, the dependency chain made visible. Account types, fee tables, TAN comedy.

3.2Der Versicherungs-Dschungel

A street promoter, a friend and a comparison site all disagree. Reading mission: essential (Haftpflicht) vs nonsense. Wrong picks cost in-game money, never progress.

3.3Der Brief vom Beitragsservice

The mysterious Rundfunkbeitrag letter. Official-letter reading, reply by form, and the cultural scene that explains why it exists at all.

3.4Das Paket vom Zoll

A package from home is stuck at customs. A fetch-quest across town with a Zollamt battle; teaches formal phone calls, voicemail included.

BOSSDie Formular-Hydra

A multi-form gauntlet: every completed form spawns the next until your typed accuracy cuts off all heads. Recurring review, wearing its boss costume openly.

14 · Kapitel 4 · Agentur & Zuhause

Die Jobsuche

4.1Die Orientierung

Coffee with Jonas: what do you actually want here? Decode German job titles and the m/w/d jungle on a parody job portal, and build your skills inventory (your first talking-about-yourself Redemittel).

4.2Die Stellenanzeige

Reading battle: three job ads. Requirements vs nice-to-haves, corporate Denglisch, and one red-flag ad to spot before you waste an application on it.

4.3Der Lebenslauf

Build the tabellarischer Lebenslauf: German CV conventions, the photo question, explaining gaps without panic. Typed form finale.

4.4Das Anschreiben

The cover letter, assembled from phrase blocks and then personalized (typed; connects to the app's writing coach). Confident without bragging: the register tightrope.

4.5Der Anruf

Call the company: get past the front desk, ask two smart questions about the role, and leave a voicemail that actually gets returned. Listening and speaking under gentle pressure.

4.6Die Generalprobe

Mock-interview night in the WG: Ayşe plays the merciless interviewer, Jonas coaches. Your Stärken and Schwächen cards are forged here, and you pack the bag for the boss.

BOSSDas Vorstellungsgespräch

Interview battle with Frau Weber: strengths without bragging, weaknesses without self-destruction, salary in Konjunktiv. Reward: der Arbeitsvertrag. The Büropark opens fully in the endgame career path.

15 · Kapitel 5 · Kiez & Stadtpark

Gesund & Sozial

5.1Der Arzttermin

Call the Praxis, describe symptoms with body vocabulary, navigate the Versichertenkarte ritual with Dr. Sommer.

5.2In der Apotheke

Pharmacy consultation: dosage language, the generics question, rezeptfrei vs rezeptpflichtig.

5.3Der Stammtisch

Herr Nguyen sends you to the Kneipe. A social battle night of small talk, opinions and toasts. The rare Feierabendbier item is discovered here.

5.4Der Verein

Join one: Fußball, Chor or Kleingarten. German club bureaucracy is the comedy; belonging is the reward.

5.5Das Wochenende

An S-Bahn day trip: ticket zones revisited, etiquette micro-scenes, and a Kontrolleur encounter that replays chapter-1 vocabulary.

BOSSDas Einweihungsfest

Host your own party. Written invitations, a German recipe, Krause at the door, and a surprise cameo by Frau Schmidt. The whole cast on one stage; your standing bar is renamed Gastgeber-Ehre for the night. And the next morning? The bottle mountain is the biggest Pfand payday in the game.

16 · Kapitel 6 · Endgame

Mein Ziel: you choose the ending

After the party, the game asks what a good life here means to you. Each goal is a mission chain that hardens everything: thinner D/E, richer registers, higher input rungs.

ADie Karriere: Im Büro

Office life across the ten workplace themes: the first day (du/Sie minefield), the meeting, the difficult customer, the project fight, the Dienstreise with its delayed ICE. Boss: der unbefristete Vertrag, the contract-and-salary negotiation with Frau Weber.

BDie Traumwohnung

The full apartment hunt again, solo, at real difficulty.

CDie Einbürgerung

The paperwork summit, with optional Einbürgerungstest content.

DDas eigene Projekt

Found a Verein or Kiez initiative. You become the helper for a newcomer: the role-reversal ending, with Jonas cheering.

EDie Prüfung

Optional: telc B2 or Goethe B2 as ONE selectable goal for players who want it. Never the default, never the spine of the story.

17 · Nebenquests

Side quests and the real world

  • Wort-Safari: photograph a sign or record a voice note using a freshly learned word out in the real world. The card gets a personal badge and a memory boost. Photos stay on your device only.
  • Kiez-Aufträge: Herr Nguyen's rotating mini-games: listening snippets, speed-sorting der/die/das crates, Pfand-return arithmetic.
  • Fundstücke: cultural collectibles hidden in scenes. A shelf in your flat displays them; your flat slowly becoming „yours" is the ambient progress display.

18 · Stil & Ton

Warm, funny, on your side

  • Tone: the game lovingly roasts German bureaucracy, never the learner. Humor is situational, not text-wall jokes.
  • Visuals (blessed): committed 2D pixel art in its MODERN form. Your approved reference is mockup scene 7 (muted contemporary palette, app-language UI, crisp half-size UI pixels over the chunky pixel world); the GBA-retro styling was rejected as dated. All game art selects against that reference.
  • Language: scene German is level-banded to the player; interface text stays minimal; D and E sit on every content line. No em dashes anywhere.

Pixel props above are code-drawn placeholders, not the proposed art style.
