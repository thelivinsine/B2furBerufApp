Identity Exploration · Genauly

Vol. III · three new philosophies

A logo is easy. Decide what the brand believes first.

The first ten directions all stood on one idea: genau, precision, being correct. That is a real foundation, but it is not the only one. Below are three different beliefs about what Genauly is actually for, each with its own set of marks. Pick the sentence that feels most true and the visual language follows from it. Same spec per direction: mark, palette, type, and where it wins.

Philosophy Aankommen · to arrive, to settle in

The goal was never grammar. It was belonging.

Nobody learns German for the dative case. They learn it to be understood at the doctor, to get the flat, to be included at the table. If the brand believes this, it stops looking like a study tool and starts looking like a door held open. Warm, human, and about the person on the other side of the language, not the language itself.

Philosophy Bdurchbruch · breakthrough

The whole product exists to end one specific stuckness.

This is the sharpest strategic truth you have: learners are trapped on the B1 to B2 plateau, and Genauly is the thing that gets them off it. A brand built on this belief is about threshold and motion, the line you break through, the leap you finally make. It is the most energetic, most product-honest foundation of the three.

Philosophy Cklarheit · clarity

Intermediate German is fog. We are the moment it clears.

At B1 you understand roughly half of everything, which is its own kind of exhausting. The product's real gift is the click when the fog lifts: the sentence that suddenly parses, the announcement you finally catch. A brand on this belief is about light and comprehension, turning noise into signal. Calm, intelligent, and quietly premium.

Three beliefs, three different companies

These are not just ten more logos. Each philosophy would make Genauly a different kind of product, so the real choice is the belief, and the mark follows. Here is how I read them:

Philosophy A

Ankommen → warmth

Positions you against cold, gamified apps. Best if the mission is integration and real life. Softest, most human.

Philosophy B

Durchbruch → momentum

The sharpest marketing story: it names the exact pain (the plateau) and sells the escape. Most energetic, most direct.

Philosophy C

Klarheit → intelligence

The most premium and defensible. Positions Genauly as the smart, calm tool for understanding, not drilling.

My instinct: Durchbruch is the strongest story (it owns the plateau, which is your whole reason to exist), while Ankommen / Schwelle is the warmest feeling and the furthest from every competitor. If you tell me which belief rings true, I will pair it with the sharpest mark in its set, lock a single spec, wire the tokens into the app, and regenerate every icon.
