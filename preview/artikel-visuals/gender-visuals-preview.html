Visual gender cues for the vocabulary bank

Seven ideas from the discussion, mocked up in the app's real tokens (light and dark follow your theme, same as the app). All words are real entries from the bank. Numbers match the idea list from the chat; the recommended ship order is at the bottom.

01

ArticleBadge color coding

Ship first Effort: low Impact: high

One shared chip, used on every surface that shows a noun. Der is blue, die is rose, das is green (the textbook convention). Below: how it lands on the Karten grid, the flashcard, and the Liste view, plus three tint variants to pick from.

02

Triple encoding: color + shape + text

Ship with 01 Effort: minimal Accessibility

A tiny shape inside the chip so gender never relies on color alone: square for der, circle for die, triangle for das. The grayscale strip simulates what a colorblind learner sees; the shapes still carry the information.

04

Gender rings in the Wörter graph

Effort: medium Pattern discovery

Node fill stays the domain color (unchanged). Nouns gain a thin gender-colored ring; verbs and adjectives get none, so the ring doubles as a part-of-speech cue. Zoomed out, "-ung ist immer die" becomes literally visible as a rose cluster.

05

Gender-pattern hints

Effort: low Teaches the rule

A pure helper detects rule-governed suffixes (-ung, -heit, -keit, -schaft → die; -chen → das; agent -er → der) and shows a one-line hint under the chip. Every matching card becomes a micro grammar lesson, across all 1,246 words, with zero content authoring.

06

Gender-sort mini-game (Neuland scene kind)

Effort: high G2 candidate

A new scene kind for the game layer: words appear and the player taps them into the matching door under light time pressure. Fits the G2 "scene variety" priority and reuses the same three gender colors, so the app and the game teach one visual language. Mocked in the pixel-UI style (in-mission scenes are light-only by design).

07

Sub-theme icon watermark

Effort: low Context, not gender

The 80% version of per-word imagery without licensing 1,246 pictures: the sub-theme's icon as a faint watermark in the card corner, giving situational grounding (this word lives in "meetings", that one in "arzt.termin"). Composable with the ArticleBadge.

Recommended ship order

  1. 01 + 02 together: one shared ArticleBadge (pastel variant A, with shapes), dropped into Karten, Tabelle, Liste, flashcards, session blocks, and the graph's selected-node card. About a day of work, immediately visible everywhere.
  2. 03 + 05: pure helpers over existing data (plural diff, suffix rules). No content edits, no new assets.
  3. 04: the graph ring, once the three tints are locked as a convention.
  4. 06: park as a G2 scene-kind candidate alongside the Keypad/Automat rung.
  5. 07: optional garnish; skip the per-word imagery version entirely.
